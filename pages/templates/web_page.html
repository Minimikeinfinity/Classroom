Characteristics of a FRACTION:

When the numerator is less than the denominator, the fraction is proper, i.e., less than unity.

When the reverse is true, e.g., 5/2, the fraction is improper, i.e., greater than unity.

When a fraction is written with a whole number, e.g., 31/2, the expression is called a mixed number.

(The present notation for fractions is of Hindu origin, but some types of fractions were used by the Egyptians before 1600 BC Another way of representing fractions is by decimal notation.)
